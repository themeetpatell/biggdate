# BiggDate Brand Book Handbook

Version: 1.0.0  
Date: 2026-05-01  
Owner: Founders + Product + Growth

## 1. Brand North Star

BiggDate exists to replace low-signal dating chaos with high-signal relationship clarity.

Brand promise:
- We respect your time.
- We surface real compatibility.
- We help you date with intention, not noise.

Tagline:
- See your future, not just a profile.

Working mantra:
- Dating that respects your time.

## 2. Positioning

Category:
- Relationship-intelligence dating platform.

Primary differentiation:
- Not a swipe volume app.
- Not a vibe-only matching app.
- Not generic personality-test theater.

Positioning statement:
- For busy, thoughtful people tired of low-signal dating loops, BiggDate is the dating product that combines deep profile context, intentional matching, and guided relationship clarity so every interaction has a better chance of becoming real.

## 3. Audience and Mindsets

### Primary audience
- Founders, operators, and ambitious professionals with limited time.
- People who are emotionally serious but allergic to performative dating behavior.

### Secondary audience
- Growth-minded daters who value reflection, coaching, and compatibility depth.

### Core emotional jobs
- Reduce dating fatigue.
- Increase confidence before meeting.
- Filter misalignment early.
- Preserve emotional energy and calendar quality.

## 4. Brand Personality

BiggDate should feel:
- Sharp
- Warm
- Confident
- Unapologetically honest
- High-standard

BiggDate should never feel:
- Corporate
- Fluffy
- Cynical
- Desperate
- Pretentious

Voice archetype:
- The brutally caring strategist: tells the truth clearly, then helps users move forward.

## 5. Tone System

### Default tone
- Direct, grounded, emotionally intelligent.
- One notch bolder than typical dating apps.

### Situational tone modes

1. Product explanation:
- Clear and practical.
- Focus on user outcomes, not feature jargon.

2. Motivation and launch:
- Energetic and conviction-led.
- Punchy language is okay when tied to user truth.

3. Sensitive moments (rejection, safety, errors):
- Calm, respectful, non-judgmental.
- Zero sarcasm.

4. Premium conversion moments:
- Confident and value-focused.
- No fear tactics or shame framing.

### Tone sliders
- Boldness: 7/10
- Warmth: 7/10
- Formality: 3/10
- Humor: 4/10 (contextual, never cruel)

## 6. Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Time is a relationship asset
Core message:
- Your time is not disposable, and your dating product should treat it like a premium resource.

- Honest signals reduce emotional ambiguity and save people from avoidable pain.

## 7. Brand Lexicon

### Words we own
- High-signal dating
- Relationship clarity
- Intentional matching
- Dating intel
- Soul Knock
- Compatibility signals
- Calendar debt
- Real-life fit

### Words we avoid
- Perfect match
- Soulmate guarantee
- Effortless love hacks
- Viral dating tricks
- Any language that implies emotional manipulation

### Signature phrasing patterns
- Contrast pairs: less noise, more signal.
- Adult-life framing: work week, real schedules, emotional bandwidth.
- Truth-forward lines: say what users already know but rarely hear from products.

## 8. Copywriting Rules

### Core rules
1. Lead with user reality, not product bragging.
2. Use concrete language over abstract claims.
3. Keep sentences short and decisive.
4. Avoid startup cliches and AI hype wording.
5. Every line should either clarify, motivate, or convert.

### Mechanical style
- Sentence case by default.
- Short paragraphs.
- Strong verbs.
- Avoid exclamation overload.
- Prefer specific nouns over adjectives.

### CTA style
- Outcome-oriented:
  - See my matches
  - Answer the knock
  - Open chat
  - Start onboarding
- Avoid vague CTA labels:
  - Learn more
  - Continue
  - Click here

## 9. Voice Examples

### Better
- You are not bad at dating. You are just tired of low-signal loops.
- Before you clear your evening, see how this person actually lives.
- We optimize for fewer wrong turns, not more swipes.

### Avoid
- Unlock true love with our revolutionary AI engine.
- Find your perfect match instantly.
- Increase your match ratio by 300 percent.

## 10. Visual Identity Direction

Visual mood:
- Cinematic, premium, emotionally charged, modern.

System cues reflected in product:
- Dark-forward gradients with neon accents.
- Magenta and electric blue as signature energy colors.
- High contrast surfaces with soft glow effects.
- Rounded geometry and layered glass-like treatments.

Visual principles:
1. Atmosphere first: backgrounds should create emotion.
2. Signal hierarchy: every screen needs a clear focal action.
3. Controlled intensity: use glow and gradients with restraint.
4. Consistency over novelty: recognizable brand rhythm across pages.

## 11. Color Intent (Brand Semantics)

- Magenta/pink: emotional energy, confidence, spark.
- Electric blue: intelligence, trust, signal.
- Cyan/mint accents: freshness and momentum.
- Deep navy/black surfaces: depth and premium tone.
- Light text on dark layers: clarity and focus.

## 12. Typography Intent

- Sans for utility and interface clarity.
- Serif/display moments for emotional emphasis and narrative weight.
- Tight, intentional heading rhythm.
- Generous line-height in body copy to reduce cognitive load.

## 13. Motion and Interaction Style

Motion personality:
- Intentional, fluid, and cinematic.

Guidelines:
- Favor purposeful transitions over decorative motion.
- Use stagger and reveal for narrative sections.
- Keep interaction feedback immediate and smooth.
- Do not over-animate high-frequency UI controls.

## 14. Brand Safety and Trust Language

Always include:
- Respectful consent framing.
- Clear reporting/block options.
- Honest explanation when moderation or limits apply.

Never include:
- Blame language toward users.
- Shame-based upsell copy.
- Manipulative urgency in emotional contexts.

## 15. Do/Do Not Matrix

Do:
- Say hard truths with care.
- Write for real-life decision making.
- Keep claims grounded in product behavior.

Do not:
- Overpromise relationship outcomes.
- Use emotionally exploitative hooks.
- Drift into generic dating-app language.

## 16. Brand Governance

Approval lanes:
- Core positioning and tagline: founders.
- Product microcopy: product + design.
- Growth campaigns and social: growth lead + founder review for major launches.

Review checklist before publishing:
1. Is this high-signal and specific?
2. Does it sound like BiggDate, not generic SaaS copy?
3. Is the emotional tone appropriate for context?
4. Is the claim provable by product behavior?
5. Is the CTA explicit and outcome-based?

## 17. Rapid Brand QA Rubric

Score each from 1 to 5:
- Clarity
- Conviction
- Human warmth
- Product truth
- Distinctiveness

Minimum publish threshold:
- Average score >= 4.0 and no category below 3.

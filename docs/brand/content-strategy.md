# BiggDate Content Strategy

Version: 1.0.0  
Date: 2026-05-01  
Owner: Growth + Founders + Product Marketing

## 1. Strategy Objective

Build a consistent content engine that:
- Attracts high-intent users aligned with BiggDate values.
- Educates users on relationship-intelligence dating.
- Converts qualified audience into onboarding starts, active usage, and paid upgrades.

## 2. Strategic Principles

1. Teach before you sell.
2. Say what other dating brands avoid saying.
3. Anchor content in real user pain and product truth.
4. Prioritize consistency over viral spikes.
5. Optimize for trust and resonance, not vanity metrics.

## 3. Content Positioning

BiggDate content angle:
- Less dating entertainment, more dating intelligence.

Narrative territory:
- Real compatibility over aesthetic matching.
- Emotional clarity over performative flirting.
- Intentional progression over endless chatting.

## 4. Content Pillars and Themes

### Pillar A: Dating Reality and Pattern Breaks
Purpose:
- Name common frustrations and reframe them with practical insight.

Theme examples:
- Why swipe volume fails busy people.
- What "good chemistry" misses in real relationships.
- The hidden cost of low-signal dating loops.

### Pillar B: Relationship Intelligence Education
Purpose:
- Build credibility through practical frameworks users can apply immediately.

Theme examples:
- Attachment style in modern dating.
- Conflict style compatibility.
- Life architecture alignment.

### Pillar C: Product-Led Proof
Purpose:
- Show exactly how BiggDate solves specific pain points.

Theme examples:
- Soul Knock and intentional conversation unlock.
- Profile depth that predicts real-life fit.
- Debrief loops and better decision quality.

### Pillar D: Founder Conviction and Brand POV
Purpose:
- Humanize brand and strengthen trust.

Theme examples:
- Why we built BiggDate.
- Product principles and hard decisions.
- What we refuse to optimize for.

### Pillar E: Community and Social Validation
Purpose:
- Showcase authentic user voices and momentum.

Theme examples:
- User stories and wins.
- Community prompts and reflection responses.
- Product feedback loops in public.

## 5. Audience Segmentation for Content

### Segment 2: Emotionally intentional daters

Message angle:

### Segment 3: Founder/operator communities
Need:
- Dating system that respects intense schedules.

Message angle:
- A relationship product designed for real calendars.

## 6. Channel Strategy

## 6.1 Instagram
Role:
- Top-of-funnel emotional resonance and shareable identity content.

Formats:
- Carousels, reels, short statement cards, stories.

Cadence:
- 4 to 6 posts/week.

Winning formats:
- Bold truths in 5-slide carousels.
- "Old dating advice vs high-signal dating" comparisons.

## 6.2 LinkedIn
Role:
- Founder-led thought leadership and professional audience conversion.

Formats:
- Founder essays, framework posts, product decision breakdowns.

Cadence:
- 2 to 3 posts/week.

Winning formats:
- Strong opinion + practical framework + product proof.

## 6.3 Twitter/X
Role:
- Fast-moving POV, product moments, and conversation loops.

Formats:
- Threads, short takes, product snippets, launch commentary.

Cadence:
- Daily short-form, 2 threads/week.

## 6.4 Medium
Role:
- Long-form authority and evergreen educational content.

Formats:
- Deep dives, framework explainers, product philosophy essays.

Cadence:
- 2 to 4 articles/month.

## 6.5 YouTube
Role:
- Trust and depth through narrative and educational video.

Formats:
- Founder talks, product walkthroughs, relationship framework explainers.

Cadence:
- 2 videos/month initially.

## 6.6 Email (Lifecycle + Product)
Role:
- Activation, retention, and conversion.

Streams:
- Onboarding nudges.
- Match-ready prompts.
- Soul Knock and mutual-match notifications.
- Premium value education.

## 7. Content Funnel Architecture

Top of funnel:
- Reality-based dating truth content.
- Goal: reach, saves, shares, profile visits.

Middle of funnel:
- Framework and education content.
- Goal: trust, return visits, waitlist/beta intent.

Bottom of funnel:
- Product proof and conversion content.
- Goal: onboarding starts, active usage, paid upgrades.

Retention:
- In-product education + lifecycle reminders + community loops.

## 8. Editorial Calendar Framework

Weekly content mix template:
- 2 posts: Dating reality and pattern breaks.
- 2 posts: Relationship intelligence education.
- 1 post: Product proof.
- 1 post: Founder POV or community signal.

Monthly campaign rhythm:
1. Week 1: Insight campaign.
2. Week 2: Product proof campaign.
3. Week 3: Community spotlight campaign.
4. Week 4: Conversion and recap campaign.

## 9. Content Production Workflow

1. Ideation:
- Pull ideas from support questions, onboarding drop-offs, pulse themes, and founder notes.

2. Prioritization:
- Score by relevance, timeliness, and conversion potential.

3. Drafting:
- Build first draft with pillar alignment and clear CTA.

4. Review:
- Brand voice review and factual claim check.

5. Production:
- Design adaptation by channel format.

6. Publishing:
- Channel-specific scheduling with distribution plan.

7. Post-publish analysis:
- 24h and 7d performance review, learnings, and iteration.

## 11. Content Templates

## 11.1 Instagram Carousel Template
Slide 1:
- Contrarian truth headline.

Slides 2 to 4:
- Problem decomposition with one practical insight per slide.

Slide 5:
- Product implication + CTA.

## 11.2 LinkedIn POV Template
- Hook: unpopular but true statement.
- Context: what most products/users do today.
- Framework: 3 practical principles.
- Product tie-in: how BiggDate operationalizes it.
- CTA: conversation or onboarding link.

## 11.3 Thread Template
- Tweet 1: high-friction dating pain statement.
- Tweets 2 to 5: breakdown and examples.
- Tweet 6: framework or method.
- Tweet 7: BiggDate angle + CTA.

## 11.4 Product Launch Template
- Problem users face.
- What changed in product.
- Why this matters in real life.
- How to use it now.
- Direct CTA.

## 12. Brand Voice Guardrails in Content

Always:
- Speak like a sharp human, not a corporate playbook.
- Keep truth-forward tone with emotional intelligence.
- Use concrete examples from real dating contexts.

Never:
- Promise guaranteed romantic outcomes.
- Shame users for current behavior.
- Use manipulative relationship advice tropes.

## 14. KPIs and Measurement

Reach and awareness:
- Impressions
- Video views
- Share rate

Engagement quality:
- Saves/bookmarks
- Meaningful comments
- Completion rate for long-form assets

Conversion:
- Profile clicks
- Onboarding starts
- Signup completion
- Upgrade conversion from content-attributed users

Retention impact:
- Returning active users exposed to content
- Debrief and companion usage after educational campaigns

## 15. Experiment Roadmap

Month 1:
- Test 3 hook styles: direct truth, data-led, founder POV.

Month 2:
- Test format depth: short reels vs long carousels vs text threads.

Month 3:
- Test conversion CTA variants by audience segment.

Decision rule:
- Scale only formats with both engagement quality and conversion evidence.

## 16. Governance and Editorial Standards

Approval matrix:
- Founder POV and major positioning shifts: founder approval.
- Product claims: product/engineering factual check.
- Sensitive dating psychology content: editorial sensitivity review.

Quality checklist before publish:
1. Is this aligned to a content pillar?
2. Is the claim true in product reality?
3. Is the tone unmistakably BiggDate?
4. Is there a single clear CTA?
5. Does this respect user dignity and emotional context?

## 17. 30-Day Starter Plan

Week 1:
- Publish 2 dating-reality posts + 1 founder POV + 1 product walkthrough.

Week 2:
- Publish 2 education posts + 1 user-proof post + 1 conversion post.

Week 3:
- Publish 2 myth-busting posts + 1 framework thread + 1 pulse/community angle.

Week 4:
- Publish 2 recap/insight posts + 1 launch teaser + 1 direct onboarding CTA asset.

Post-month review:
- Keep top 20 percent of formats.
- Cut bottom 30 percent.
- Reinvest in formats that drive qualified onboarding starts.
